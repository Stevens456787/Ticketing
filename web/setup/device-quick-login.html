Configure Device Quick-Login

Device Quick-Login gives field officers a biometric or PIN shortcut once their device is trusted. Follow these steps to enable it safely.

  1. Require an initial full sign-in (email/password or SSO) to bind the device and collect device identifiers (e.g., secure hardware ID, push token).
  2. Store the refresh token and quick-login secret in the OS secure enclave (Keychain, Keystore) via the mobile app and mark the device as trusted in the backend.
  3. Enable biometric prompts (Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint) or a locally stored 6-digit PIN. Never store raw PIN values; use salted hashes.
  4. Expose a device management screen so administrators can revoke quick-login access remotely.
  5. During login attempts, validate device trust server-side and issue short-lived session tokens.
Security reminder: enforce automatic revocation after repeated biometric failures and when the officer reports a lost device.
